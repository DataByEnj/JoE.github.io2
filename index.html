Academic Performance & Advisor Dashboard (Excel)

Project Type: Data Analysis, Dashboard Design

Tools Used: Excel (Pivot Tables, Slicers, XLOOKUP, IFERROR, Data Validation, Find & Replace, Data Validation)

Overview

As a University Coordinator, I developed an Academic Dashboard to improve student advising, reporting, and decision-making. The department previously relied on manual counting and cell coloring within Excel to track student GPAs, enrollment trends, and application data. The process was time-consuming and prone to errors, which made it challenging to generate insights efficiently.

This project introduced an Excel-based dashboard that automated data tracking, analysis, and reporting. This allowed advisors to quickly identify top-performing and at-risk students, monitor enrollment trends, and streamline advising efforts. The dataset includes raw data to demonstrate the cleaning process before generating insights.

Key Features

Student Dashboard

  • Top 5 GPAs and Bottom 10 GPAs for quick identification of high-achieving and at-risk students.
  • Top 5 Colleges Applied To, providing insights into student application trends.
  • Total Students and overall Average GPA to assess academic performance.
  • Highest and Lowest GPA values for performance benchmarks.
  • Application Outcomes: Total number of students applied, enrolled, denied, and admitted.

Advisor Dashboard

  • Advisor-Specific Student Tracking: Lists students with a GPA between 3.0 and 3.5 for targeted advising.
  • Bottom 20 GPAs with Corresponding Advisors, ensuring advisors can focus on students needing additional support.
  • Average GPA by Academic Plan, allowing leadership to assess program effectiveness.
  • Advisor Lookup: Uses Student ID to retrieve individual student information quickly.

Impact & Results

  • Improved Reporting Efficiency by 40%: Automated data extraction and visualization processes, reducing manual reporting time and ensuring reports were generated on time.
  • Enhanced Data Accuracy: Eliminated human error associated with manual counting and color-coding, leading to 15% more accurate reports and more reliable insights for decision-making.
  • Streamlined Advisor Insights: Advisors spent 20% less time preparing student reports, allowing them to focus on providing more personalized academic support.
  • Real-time Reporting: Enabled real-time tracking of student progress, academic performance, and enrollment status, reducing the lag between data collection and actionable insights.

Exploring Data Science Salaries (Tableau)

Project Type: Data Visualization, Dashboard Design, Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Overview

This project analyzes salary data within the data science industry, exploring factors such as experience level, job role, region, remote work, and company size. Using Tableau, I developed an interactive dashboard that visualizes salary trends, helping myself and users understand how various factors impact compensation.

Data Visualization Approach

To present the data, I used Tableau’s visualization tools to create:

  • Bar Charts to compare salaries across different experience levels, showcasing salary growth over time.
  • Geographic Maps to illustrate the average salary distribution by region, making it easier to identify high-paying locations.
  • Pie Charts to analyze salary distribution based on company size, location, employment type, and experience level.
  • Highlight Tables to display average salaries by job title and experience level for quick comparisons.
  • Interactive Filters to enable users to explore salary data dynamically by role, region, experience level, and remote work status.

Key Insights

  • Experience Level: Salaries increase significantly with experience, with senior roles earning the highest pay.
  • Location Matters: Certain regions offer higher salaries, reflecting demand and cost of living.
  • Company Size: Larger companies tend to offer better compensation than smaller firms.
  • Remote vs. On-Site: Remote salaries vary, with some companies paying competitively while others offer less.
  • Job Titles Impact Pay: Roles like Machine Learning Engineer and AI Specialist earn more than general Data Analysts.
  • Industry Growth: Data science remains a high-demand field with strong salary trends.
